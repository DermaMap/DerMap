# Chemical Peels

> **Category:** Skin Resurfacing Treatment  
> **Treatment Time:** 20–60 minutes  
> **Downtime:** Depends on peel depth  
> **Results:** Improved texture, tone, and radiance  
> **Best For:** Pigmentation, uneven texture, acne, fine lines

---

# Overview

A chemical peel is a professional skin resurfacing treatment that uses carefully selected chemical solutions to exfoliate the outer layers of the skin. This process stimulates skin renewal and reveals smoother, brighter, and more even-looking skin.

Chemical peels vary in strength and formulation depending on the individual's skin concerns and treatment goals.

---

# How It Works

Chemical peels work by applying a controlled chemical solution to the skin.

The solution causes controlled exfoliation, which helps:

- Remove damaged surface skin cells
- Accelerate skin renewal
- Improve skin texture
- Stimulate collagen production (especially deeper peels)
- Reduce uneven pigmentation

The depth and effect of a peel depend on the type of acid, concentration, and application technique.

---

# Types of Chemical Peels

## Superficial Peels

These affect the outermost layer of the skin.

Common ingredients:

- Glycolic Acid
- Lactic Acid
- Salicylic Acid
- Mandelic Acid

Common uses:

- Dull skin
- Mild pigmentation
- Rough texture
- Mild acne concerns

---

## Medium Peels

These penetrate deeper into the skin.

Common ingredient:

- Trichloroacetic Acid (TCA)

Common uses:

- Fine lines
- Moderate pigmentation
- Sun damage
- Uneven skin tone

---

## Deep Peels

These reach deeper layers of the skin.

Common ingredient:

- Phenol-based formulations

Used for:

- Significant photoaging
- Deep wrinkles
- Severe sun damage

Deep peels require specialized medical supervision.

# Who Should Avoid Chemical Peels

Treatment may not be recommended for individuals with:

- Active skin infections
- Open wounds
- Active cold sores
- Severe eczema flare
- Severe rosacea flare
- Recent aggressive cosmetic procedures
- Certain medications that increase skin sensitivity

A professional skin assessment is recommended before treatment.

# Procedure Steps

## 1. Skin Assessment

The provider evaluates:

- Skin type
- Skin sensitivity
- Current skincare products
- Treatment goals

---

## 2. Skin Preparation

The skin is cleansed to remove oils and impurities.

---

## 3. Chemical Solution Application

The selected peel solution is carefully applied to the treatment area.

The patient may feel:

- Tingling
- Warmth
- Mild stinging

---

## 4. Neutralization or Removal

Depending on the peel type, the solution may be neutralized or removed after a specific time.

---

## 5. Post-Treatment Care

Soothing products and sunscreen are applied to protect the skin.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Does a chemical peel hurt?

Most superficial peels cause mild tingling or warmth. Stronger peels may cause more discomfort.

---

## How long does peeling last?

It depends on the peel depth. Superficial peels may cause light peeling for several days.

---

## Can chemical peels treat acne scars?

Some peels can improve certain types of acne scars and texture, especially superficial discoloration.

---

## How often can I get a chemical peel?

Frequency depends on the peel strength and skin condition. A professional can recommend the appropriate schedule.

---

## Can I use skincare products after a peel?

Gentle hydration and sunscreen are usually recommended. Active ingredients should only be restarted according to professional guidance.
