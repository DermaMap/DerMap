# Mesotherapy

> **Category:** Injectable Skin & Scalp Treatment  
> **Treatment Time:** 20–60 minutes  
> **Downtime:** Minimal  
> **Results:** Gradual improvement depending on formulation and treatment goal  
> **Best For:** Skin hydration, texture improvement, selected pigmentation concerns, scalp support

---

# Overview

Mesotherapy is a minimally invasive cosmetic procedure that involves injecting small amounts of customized substances into the skin or scalp.

The treatment was originally developed in France and is now used in aesthetic medicine for different purposes, including skin rejuvenation and hair-related treatments.

The ingredients used vary depending on the patient's goals and may include substances such as hyaluronic acid, vitamins, antioxidants, peptides, or other medically approved components.

---

# How It Works

Mesotherapy delivers targeted ingredients into the superficial layers of the skin.

Depending on the formulation, the treatment may aim to:

- Improve skin hydration
- Support skin quality
- Enhance overall radiance
- Improve the appearance of fine lines
- Support scalp health

The effects depend on:

- The ingredients used
- Injection technique
- Skin condition
- Number of sessions

# Who Should Avoid Mesotherapy

Treatment may not be recommended for individuals with:

- Active skin infections
- Open wounds
- Certain allergies
- Bleeding disorders
- Certain medical conditions
- Unrealistic expectations

A consultation with a qualified healthcare professional is recommended.

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is mesotherapy the same as fillers?

No. Fillers add volume and structure, while mesotherapy typically delivers small amounts of active ingredients into superficial skin layers.

---

## Does mesotherapy hurt?

Most patients experience mild discomfort because very fine needles are used.

---

## How long do results last?

Results depend on the ingredients used, lifestyle, skincare routine, and maintenance treatments.

---

## Can mesotherapy replace skincare?

No. It is generally considered a complementary aesthetic procedure and does not replace daily skincare.

---

## Can mesotherapy treat hair loss?

It may be used as a supportive treatment for selected cases, but the cause of hair loss should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.
