# PRP Therapy (Platelet-Rich Plasma)

> **Category:** Regenerative Skin & Hair Treatment  
> **Treatment Time:** 30–60 minutes  
> **Downtime:** Minimal  
> **Results:** Gradual improvement over several weeks to months  
> **Best For:** Hair thinning, skin rejuvenation, texture improvement

---

# Overview

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is a regenerative treatment that uses a patient's own blood components to support natural healing and tissue regeneration.

During the procedure, a small amount of blood is collected, processed to concentrate platelets, and then injected or applied to targeted areas.

Platelets contain growth factors that play an important role in tissue repair and regeneration.

---

# How It Works

PRP therapy uses concentrated platelets from the patient's own blood.

Platelets release biological signals called growth factors that may support:

- Tissue repair
- Collagen production
- Cellular regeneration
- Improved circulation in treated areas

The goal is to stimulate the body's natural repair mechanisms.

---

# Common Applications

## Skin Rejuvenation

PRP may be used to improve:

- Skin texture
- Fine lines
- Overall skin quality
- Skin hydration appearance

---

## Hair Restoration

PRP is commonly studied and used as a supportive treatment for certain types of hair thinning.

It may help:

- Support hair follicle activity
- Improve scalp environment
- Increase hair thickness in some individuals

# Ideal Candidates

PRP may be suitable for individuals who:

- Want a regenerative approach
- Have early signs of skin aging
- Experience certain types of hair thinning
- Prefer treatments using their own biological material

---

# Who Should Avoid PRP

PRP may not be recommended for individuals with:

- Certain blood disorders
- Active infections
- Severe platelet abnormalities
- Some chronic medical conditions
- Unrealistic expectations

A healthcare professional should evaluate suitability before treatment.

---

# Procedure Steps

## 1. Consultation

The provider evaluates:

- Treatment goals
- Medical history
- Skin or scalp condition
- Expectations

---

## 2. Blood Collection

A small amount of blood is drawn, similar to a routine blood test.

---

## 3. Centrifugation

The blood sample is processed in a centrifuge to separate and concentrate platelet-rich plasma.

---

## 4. PRP Preparation

The concentrated plasma containing platelets and growth factors is prepared for application.

---

## 5. Injection or Application

PRP may be:

- Injected into targeted skin areas
- Applied with selected procedures such as microneedling
- Injected into the scalp for hair-related treatments

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is PRP made from my own blood?

Yes. PRP is prepared from a patient's own blood sample.

---

## Is PRP a filler?

No. PRP does not add artificial volume like dermal fillers. It is intended to support natural regenerative processes.

---

## How quickly do results appear?

Results usually develop gradually as biological repair processes occur.

---

## Can PRP help hair loss?

PRP may support hair growth in some individuals with certain types of hair thinning. A medical evaluation is recommended to determine suitability.

---

## How long do results last?

Duration varies depending on individual factors and maintenance treatments.

---

# Evidence Summary

Research suggests PRP may have potential benefits in selected dermatologic and aesthetic applications, including certain hair loss conditions and skin rejuvenation approaches.

Results depend on patient characteristics, treatment protocol, and clinical evaluation.
