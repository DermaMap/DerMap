# Microneedling

> **Category:** Skin Rejuvenation  
> **Treatment Time:** 30–60 minutes  
> **Downtime:** 24–72 hours  
> **Results:** Gradual improvement over several weeks  
> **Best For:** Acne scars, enlarged pores, fine lines, uneven skin texture

---

# Overview

Microneedling, also known as **collagen induction therapy**, is a minimally invasive cosmetic procedure that stimulates the skin's natural healing response. Tiny sterile needles create controlled micro-injuries in the skin, encouraging the production of new collagen and elastin.

The treatment improves skin texture, firmness, elasticity, and overall appearance while preserving the integrity of the epidermis.

---

# How It Works

Microneedling creates thousands of microscopic channels in the skin.

These microchannels trigger the body's wound-healing process, leading to:

- Increased collagen synthesis
- Increased elastin production
- Improved skin regeneration
- Enhanced absorption of topical skincare products

Healing occurs naturally over the following weeks.

# Ideal Candidates

Microneedling is generally suitable for individuals who have:

- Mild to moderate acne scars
- Fine wrinkles
- Rough texture
- Large pores
- Uneven skin tone
- Early signs of aging

---

# Who Should Avoid Microneedling

Treatment should generally be postponed or avoided if the patient has:

- Active acne breakouts
- Active skin infection
- Cold sores
- Severe eczema flare
- Psoriasis flare
- Open wounds
- Recent sunburn
- History of poor wound healing
- Certain bleeding disorders

A consultation with a qualified healthcare professional is recommended before treatment.

# Procedure Steps

## 1. Consultation

The skin is evaluated to determine suitability.

---

## 2. Cleansing

The treatment area is thoroughly cleansed.

---

## 3. Numbing Cream

A topical anesthetic is usually applied for approximately 20–30 minutes.

---

## 4. Microneedling

A professional microneedling device is moved across the treatment area using sterile disposable needle cartridges.

Treatment depth varies depending on the skin concern.

---

## 5. Serum Application

Hydrating or soothing serums may be applied immediately afterward.

Common ingredients include:

- Hyaluronic Acid
- Panthenol
- Copper Peptides

---

## 6. Recovery

Skin appears pink or red for one to three days.

---

# Recommended Number of Sessions

General recommendations:

| Concern | Sessions |
|---------|----------|
| Skin rejuvenation | 3–4 |
| Acne scars | 4–6 |

---

# Possible Side Effects

Common temporary effects include:

- Redness
- Swelling
- Dryness
- Mild peeling
- Tenderness

These effects usually resolve within a few days.

---

# Rare Risks

Although uncommon, possible complications include:

- Infection
- Hyperpigmentation
- Prolonged redness
- Allergic reaction to topical products

Proper sterile technique reduces these risks.

---

# Aftercare

For the first 24–48 hours:

✔ Use gentle cleanser

✔ Apply moisturizer

✔ Stay hydrated

✔ Avoid touching the face unnecessarily

✔ Use broad-spectrum SPF daily

Avoid:

- Retinoids
- AHAs
- BHAs
- Vitamin C (strong formulations)
- Saunas
- Swimming pools
- Heavy exercise
- Direct sun exposure

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is microneedling painful?

Most patients experience only mild discomfort due to topical numbing cream.

---

## When will I see results?

Initial improvements may appear within one to two weeks, while collagen production continues for several months.

---

## Can microneedling treat acne scars?

Yes. It is commonly used for mild to moderate atrophic acne scars.

---

## Can it be combined with other treatments?

Many providers combine microneedling with selected topical serums. Combination treatments should be performed only by qualified professionals.

---

## How long do results last?

Results vary depending on skin condition, age, lifestyle, and skincare routine. Maintenance sessions may help preserve improvements.
