What is Laser Resurfacing?

Laser resurfacing uses concentrated light beams to remove the outer layers of damaged skin and stimulate collagen production in the deeper layers. It's a powerful treatment for reversing signs of aging, acne scarring, and sun damage.

Benefits

  • Reduces fine lines, wrinkles, and crow's feet
  • Improves acne and surgical scars
  • Corrects sun damage and age spots
  • Tightens loose skin
  • Improves overall skin texture and tone
  • Stimulates long-term collagen production

Types of Lasers

  • Ablative Lasers (CO2, Erbium): Remove outer skin layers. More dramatic results but longer downtime.
  • Non-Ablative Lasers: Heat underlying tissue without damaging the surface. Less downtime, requires multiple sessions.

Recovery Time

Recovery varies by laser type. Non-ablative treatments may require 3-5 days of redness. Ablative treatments require 1-2 weeks of significant healing, including redness, swelling, and peeling. Strict sun avoidance is mandatory.

What to Expect

  • Duration: 30 minutes to 2 hours
  • Sensation: Numbing cream or local anesthesia is used
  • Results: Immediate skin tightening, full results in 3-6 months
  • Sessions: 1-3 sessions depending on laser type
